# Lessons

- Stop re-submitting a vetoed proposal unchanged: UNH, JPM, and XOM were each proposed twice and vetoed twice within hours — either fix the parameter the risk agent flagged or drop the name, don't burn cycles re-pitching identical size/thesis.
- The only approved trade (AAPL pullback-long) was the sole proposal that matched the pre-vetted premarket watchlist; the six vetoed ideas (XOM/UNH/JPM) were off-list improvisations — anchor proposals to names researched premarket rather than chasing intraday breakouts.
- Graded counterfactuals (2026-07-20..22, 9 proposals): passing on them was worth -$336.10 in aggregate, so the risk filter is NOT too tight. The small misses (XOM +120/+55, JPM +69/+39) are outweighed by one save: the META relative-strength-long the risk agent APPROVED and the deterministic guardrail rejected on notional/cost would have lost -$740. When the LLM and the guardrail disagree, the guardrail has the better record so far.
- UNH trend-breakout-long lost money on both attempts (-$37.84, -$31.79): a name being "green on the day" is not evidence the entry was good. Judge a veto by entry-to-exit P&L at the proposed size, never by the day's open-to-close move — that confusion produced a lesson here claiming UNH was a winner.
